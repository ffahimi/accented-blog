On Building

April 2026

Many things worth building begin in an unimpressive form. They are too small to look serious and too incomplete to impress anyone. This is one reason most people stop early.

They want the first version to look like the final version. But the first version has a different job. Its job is to exist.

Once something exists, even in rough form, it changes the conversation. You can test it, revise it, and see where the energy gathers around it. Before that, all you have is mood.

Builders often overestimate the value of intensity and underestimate the value of return. It matters less that you work in one heroic burst than that you come back again the next day.

Good things are often built by people who are willing to remain interested longer than others.
