On Building

May 2026

Many useful things begin in a form that does not look serious. They are too small to impress anyone and too unfinished to explain themselves. This is one reason most people stop early.

They want the first version to resemble the final one. But the first version has a different job. Its job is simply to exist.

Once something exists, even roughly, the conversation changes. You can test it, revise it, and notice where the energy gathers around it. Before that, you mostly have mood.

Builders often overestimate the value of intensity and underestimate the value of return. It matters less that you work in one heroic burst than that you come back again tomorrow.
