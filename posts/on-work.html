On Work

May 2026

People often describe work in moral language before they describe it in practical language. They ask whether someone is disciplined, ambitious, serious. These matter, but less than they seem.

A lot of useful work comes from sustained curiosity. You notice something others ignore, and you keep returning to it. Over time, attention compounds. Eventually it starts to look like expertise.

Some of the best signals are quiet ones: what you read when nobody asks, what problems keep pulling you back, what kind of difficulty feels alive rather than draining.

Work gets easier when you stop trying to want what sounds impressive and start building around what genuinely holds your attention.
