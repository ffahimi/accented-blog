On Work

April 2026

One of the strange things about work is that people often describe it in moral terms before they describe it in practical ones. They ask whether someone is disciplined, ambitious, serious. Those things matter, but not as much as they seem.

A lot of useful work comes from sustained curiosity. You notice something others ignore, and you keep returning to it. Over time, attention accumulates. Eventually it becomes expertise.

This means that some of the best signals are quiet ones: what you read when nobody is asking, what problems you circle back to, what kind of difficulty feels alive rather than draining.

Work gets much easier when you stop trying to want what sounds impressive and start building around what genuinely holds your attention.

The real advantage is not intensity. It is durability.
