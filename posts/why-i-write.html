Why I Write

April 2026

Writing is usually presented as expression. But most of the time it is compression. You begin with something oversized and vague in your head, and the act of writing forces it into a form that can survive outside you.

This is why writing feels harder than talking. Speech can hide confusion inside speed. Writing cannot. On the page, every weak sentence exposes an unfinished thought.

I write partly to discover what I think. But that is only half of it. The other half is that writing creates a standard. Once something is written clearly, it becomes harder to lie to yourself about it later.

The internet encourages constant reaction. Writing, at its best, does the opposite. It asks you to remain with a question long enough to make it useful.

That is reason enough to keep doing it.
