Why I Write

May 2026

Writing is often described as expression, but most of the time it feels more like compression. You begin with something vague and oversized in your mind, and the work is to reduce it to a shape that can survive on the page.

This is why writing is harder than talking. Speech can move quickly enough to hide confusion. Writing cannot. A weak sentence is usually a weak thought that lost its disguise.

I write partly to find out what I think. But that is only half of it. The other half is that writing creates a standard. Once something has been said clearly, it becomes harder to lie to yourself about it later.

The internet encourages reaction. Writing, at its best, encourages remaining with a question long enough to make it useful.
