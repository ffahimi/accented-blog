Disassociation

May 2026

In the city where everybody has everything, or thinks they have everything, we have so many thoughts to shuffle through that we can’t prioritize them.

Nobody wants heavy thoughts. Nobody wants association with heavy thoughts. No one wants even the slightest association with what they see as heavy thoughts.

They prioritize happiness because they are so sad and lonely.

And the loneliness is because they don’t want to go deep into the pain.

They see their pain reflected in the outside world, and they disassociate from it.

It is a vicious circle. It never ends.

Loneliness comes from sadness, sadness comes from loneliness, and loneliness comes from disassociation.

And the ones who give us the truth — well, the ones who give us the truth are the ones with the novel.
